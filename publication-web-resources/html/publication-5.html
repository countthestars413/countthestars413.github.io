A Poetic Approach to Climate Change

Growing Rainbows from the Shadows

10

This small, shady schoolyard site is brought to life with all-season colour.

In fall, returning students are welcomed with blazing red and golden foliage. Amidst the dullness of winter, gold, ruby, and violet dogwood stems and evergreen shrubs pop with brightness, while strange seedheads spark botanical curiosity. Spring emerges with cheerful blossoms and pendulous catkins.

This space is designed for students to loiter comfortably between class times. The stepping stones and rock pile respond to student input and provide space for fun and whimsy.

The schoolyard site is a challenging location. North-facing, with a building to its back, this site is dark and shady. Maintenance is low, so plants need to be drought-tolerant to survive the Calgary climate.

Despite these challenges, I wanted to ensure the site popped with colour to keep it cheerful year-round. Dogwoods do the heavy lifting in the winter, with the brightest red, gold, and purple-stemmed cultivars chosen. Shade-tolerant yews provide a green backdrop, while dwarf conifers needing a little more light brighten the sunnier side. Trees were also chosen to be sturdy and fun to climb.
