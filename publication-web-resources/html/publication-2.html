This project was part of a work-integrated studio completed at Stantec.

In the re-design of local Tompkins Park, we were challenged to follow mock client goals, familiarize ourselves with typical landscape design workflows, and deep-dive into the research of hardscape materials, site furnishings, and planting design.

The concept was inspired by both the feeling of the site itself, and the park dedication, which states the purpose of the site is to be a quiet and peaceful refuge. Though distinctly urban, the soft forms, dense plantings, and dark colour palette present the suggestion of a deep woodland escape.

A Poetic Approach to Climate Change

This design aimed to create a whimsical, enchanted space that harmoniously blended the surrounding character of the neighbourhood with a dense woodland feeling. I wanted to create a bubble outside of time, and a peaceful, flexible space to rest, gather, and reflect in.

An urban park is subject to the limitations of high traffic and CPTED considerations. This design takes a thoughtful, deliberate approach to leverage the full potential of these restrictions. Concrete and stone are transformed into flowing water through form, contrast, colour, and shine. Site furnishings and plantings lend support in softening the hardscape.
