A history book of the people and culture of the Bowness neighbourhood tells tales a resilient, tight-knit community with a strong determination towards happiness.

When devastating floods filled the streets, children came out to play in the water. When teenagers were vandalizing the local theatre, management involved them in activities where they could develop a sense of pride and respect for the place. In winter storms, people would keep their porch lights on for the trolley drivers to see if the tram had come off the rails.

This design honours the playful, creative, and supportive heart of Bowness.
