A Poetic Approach to Climate Change

Solastalgia Sanctuaries explores the ecological grief concept of solastalgia; the feeling of homesickness when it is our home that is in fact leaving us.

A cross-Canada series of botanical gardens act as landscape time capsules, keeping the memories and stories of place alive as climate change and ecological degradation transform them before our eyes.

In an unpredictable world where the future is uncertain, we need anchor points of stillness where we can take the time to acknowledge loss and change before continuing to hurtle forward.

Each of the 10 gardens in the series represents an iconic ecoregion found along the Trans-Canada highway, presenting a representative cross-section of the landscape.. This linkage creates a familiar path along which subtle changes might become more apparent.

Climate change is expected to shift plant communities north. To predict the future plant communities surrounding the gardens, a reference cross section was chosen along the I-80, with comparison sites in ecoregions of the same Level II classification (as defined by the EPA ecoregion classification system). Landscapes were interpreted in representative snapshots.
