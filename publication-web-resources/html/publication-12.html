A Poetic Approach to Climate Change

The Urban Parking Lot as an Alvar Analog

24

Alvars are incredibly biodiverse and harsh ecosystems. Plants grow on limestone bedrock or thin soil and must tolerate extreme flooding, drought, exposure, and salts. These conditions are quite similar to a Calgary parking lot.

This design proposes an incredibly low-cost, low-maintenance solution to the challenges of greenspace in downtown cores by planting an alvar garden in an underused parking lot.

Existing pavement is cracked and A-gravel loosened. Up to 10cm of sand and peat moss is added for grassland areas. Recycled concrete barriers are refashioned into alvar cliffs.
